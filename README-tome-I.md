Tome I establishes the foundational architecture of Socioplastics as a transdisciplinary urban theory and epistemic infrastructure. Across one thousand nodes organised into ten books and one hundred chapters, it converts relational, curatorial, architectural, and urban practice into a machine-readable knowledge corpus. The tome begins with Epistemic Architecture and closes with Synthetic Infrastructure and Core II, forming the sealed foundational stratum for the developmental and expansive tomes that follow.

The corpus is organised as a nested scalar structure: Node → Book → Tome → Corpus. Each node is a discrete conceptual unit. One hundred nodes form a Book. Ten Books form this Tome.

## Core Layers — Tome I

Tome I contains two DOI-anchored ontological cores. Core I is the foundational protocol layer (Nodes 501–510). Core II is the consolidation layer that seals the tome and opens the field (Nodes 991–1000). Together they form less than 2% of the tome's nodes — the load-bearing structural anchors of the entire corpus.
